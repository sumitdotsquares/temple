      • February, 2022

        Dave Futer and Daniel Szyld were part of the AMS effort for launching a policy of double anonymous peer review.

        In March 2022 the AMS is launching a policy of double anonymous peer review for its journals with the goal of minimizing implicit bias. The AMS Committee that carried out this work was chaired by Professor Dave Futer representing Proceedings of the AMS. Professor Daniel Szyld, representing Mathematics of Computation, was a member of this committee as well.

                                    • September, 2022

                                      Queisser part of team awarded US-German collaboration grant on computational neuoroscience.

                                      Professor Gillian Queisser and collaborators have been awarded the grant “CRCNS Research Proposal: Collaborative Research: US-German Collaboration toward a biophysically principled network model of transcranial magnetic stimulation (TMS)”.

                                        • November, 2022

                                          Mitrea and collaborators publish five volume series.

                                          Professor Irina Mitrea and collaborators have published the five volume series Geometric Harmonic Analysis in Springer Nature’s “Developments in Mathematics”. 
                                          (Volume I is our now, while volumes II-V will be released over the course of the next several months.)


                                          The series captures years of original research and introduces a wide spectrum of novel geometric measure theoretic and harmonic analysis techniques into the study of general boundary value problems. 

                                          A truly major achievement - congratulations Irina.
